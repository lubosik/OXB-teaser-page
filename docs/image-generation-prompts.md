# Image Generation Prompts for Portfolio Projects

These prompts are optimized for Gemini 3 Nano/Banana 3 Pro to generate photo-realistic cover images. Each prompt includes detailed specifications for lighting, composition, camera angles, and styling to ensure professional, realistic results.

## PassportV3 Identity Verification Platform

**Prompt:**
```
Professional high-resolution photograph of a modern passport document open on a sleek, minimalist desk. The passport page displays a realistic photo ID with holographic security features visible under soft, diffused studio lighting. The background features a subtle blur with warm, professional tones. Camera angle: slightly elevated, 45-degree perspective. Depth of field: shallow focus on the passport, background softly blurred. Lighting: natural window light mixed with soft fill lighting, creating gentle shadows and highlights. Color palette: warm whites, deep navy blues, and subtle gold accents. Texture: crisp paper texture, glossy holographic elements, matte desk surface. Style: clean, professional, trustworthy, secure. No text overlays, pure photographic realism. Shot with professional camera, 85mm lens, f/2.8 aperture, natural color grading.
```

## Prompting Best Practices Used

These prompts follow photo-realistic image generation best practices:

1. **Specific Camera Specifications**: Lens focal length, aperture settings, and camera angles
2. **Detailed Lighting Descriptions**: Natural vs. artificial, direction, quality, and mood
3. **Composition Guidelines**: Camera angles, perspective, depth of field
4. **Color Palette**: Specific color descriptions for realistic rendering
5. **Texture Details**: Material descriptions (paper, chrome, wood, etc.)
6. **Style References**: Professional photography styles (editorial, architectural, etc.)
7. **Exclusion Instructions**: What NOT to include (people, text overlays, etc.)
8. **Atmosphere & Mood**: Professional descriptors that guide the overall feel

## Usage

These prompts are stored in `/data/portfolio/enhanced.ts` and can be accessed programmatically. They're also available in the CaseStudy interface as the `imagePrompt` field for reference when generating images.
